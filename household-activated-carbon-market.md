<p><p>The household activated carbon market comprises three main types: coal-based, wood-based, and coconut-based activated carbon. Coal-based activated carbon is known for its high adsorption capacity and is commonly used in air and water filtration systems. Wood-based activated carbon, derived from various types of wood, offers a higher surface area and is often used in applications where lower contaminants need removal. Coconut-based activated carbon, made from coconut shells, is highly porous and environmentally friendly, making it popular for water purification and air treatment.</p></p>
