<p><p>The passive damper market consists primarily of hydraulic and pneumatic types, both designed to reduce vibrations and enhance stability in structures and vehicles. Hydraulic dampers utilize fluid dynamics to absorb energy, making them effective for heavy-duty applications. In contrast, pneumatic dampers rely on compressed air, offering lightweight solutions with quicker response times. Each type is tailored to specific environments, with hydraulic systems often favored in industrial settings, while pneumatic options are common in automotive and aerospace industries.</p></p>
